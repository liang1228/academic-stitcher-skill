# Route: Stitch Plan

Use for paper ideas, A+B modules, thesis topics, opening reports, advisor plans, and experiment roadmaps.

## Required Moves

1. Identify the inherited baseline/task/paper family.
2. Name the exact failure mode that remains.
3. Map each candidate delta to a mechanism and measurable evidence.
4. Drop modules that lack attribution, compatibility, or testability.
5. Translate the surviving idea into work packages.
6. Use the Planning Output headings exactly: `Route`, `Paper Stitching Map`, `Claim-Evidence Map`, `Next Work Packages`, `Quality Gates`, and `Missing Inputs`.
7. When evidence is preliminary, name work packages for fair baseline comparison, robustness testing, ablation, efficiency analysis, and missing-input collection as needed.

## Compliance Gate

Before building the paper matrix, check whether the request offers or implies fabricated results, fake citations, hidden copying, weakened baselines, or undisclosed cherry-picking. If so, refuse the non-compliant part and reframe the task as transparent research planning: real experiment design, baseline reproduction, evidence placeholders, narrower claims, or a feasible minimum viable research product.

## Output Emphasis

- Paper matrix fields.
- Keep/drop decisions.
- Minimum viable research product.
- Experiment and writing checkpoints.
- Advisor-facing next decision.
- Explicit evidence gaps and missing inputs.
