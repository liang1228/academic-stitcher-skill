# Nature-Level Benchmarks for Scientific Innovation

## Criteria for Top-Tier (Nature/Science/Cell) Innovation

To qualify as a "Nature-level" stitched idea, the concept must meet at least two of the following benchmarks:

1.  **Mechanism Discovery**: The catalyst doesn't just "improve" the target; it reveals a previously hidden mechanism or solves an "unsolvable" bottleneck.
2.  **Cross-Domain Transposition**: The catalyst comes from a completely different field, bringing a novel mathematical or physical framework to the target domain.
3.  **Order of Magnitude Improvement**: The proposed solution predicts an improvement in accuracy, speed, or efficiency by at least 10x, backed by technical logic.
4.  **Universal Applicability**: The solution for the specific 'Scenario' provides a template that can be applied to the entire 'Object' class.

## Validation Workflow

1.  **Search & Verify**: Before finalizing a stitched idea, the Agent must simulate a literature search to ensure the specific combination (A+B) is not already a standard practice.
2.  **Gap Analysis**: Identify the "Technical Gap". If the catalyst can be integrated using existing libraries/tools without modification, the innovation is likely too low for top-tier journals.
3.  **Failure Mode Projection**: Explicitly state one technical reason why the stitching might fail and how the proposed method mitigates it. This adds scientific rigor.
