# Writing Quality Guidelines

## Principle: Fact-Driven Precision

The objective is to output academic prose indistinguishable from top-tier peer-reviewed literature (Nature/Science/Cell). Strictly avoid any "AI signatures" or conversational structures.

## Banned Phrases & Structural Replacements

| Avoid | Replace With |
| :--- | :--- |
| "In recent years..." | [Direct statement of historical or current technical fact] |
| "It is worth noting that..." | [State the technical fact directly without preamble] |
| "A testament to..." | "Evidence indicates that [X]..." |
| "In conclusion..." | [Directly state the forward-looking implication] |
| "In summary..." | [Directly state the forward-looking implication] |
| "Crucial" | [Specify the exact physical/chemical boundary or mechanism] |
| "Vital" | [Specify the exact physical/chemical boundary or mechanism] |
| "Essential" | [Specify the exact physical/chemical boundary or mechanism] |
| "Harnessing the power of..." | "Utilizing [X] to resolve [Y]..." |
| "Innovative" | [Describe the quantified improvement or novel mechanism] |
| "Revolutionary" | [Describe the quantified improvement or novel mechanism] |

## Sentence-Level Standards

1.  **Information Density**: Ensure every sentence introduces unique technical variables or data. Delete sentences that do not advance the physical or logical argument.
2.  **Strict Active Voice**: Use active voice to describe the proposed mechanism of stitching (e.g., "We map the catalyst's parameters onto the target's physical constraints.").
3.  **Eliminate Nominalization**: Avoid "Implementation of the optimization was achieved." Use "We optimized the lattice coordinates."
4.  **No Hype or Speculation**: Avoid qualitative modifiers. Let quantitative metrics and physical models establish the significance.
